Caroline Kessler grew up ambling and rambling through the suburbs of Baltimore, making a few forays into the Shenandoah Valley to write down the bones. While studying creative writing and religion at Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh wrapped its steely fingers around her heart. But the hold wouldn't last for long—she adventuered to south India for a semester, studying language, religion, and culture. The following summer, she worked at a human rights nonprofit in Warsaw and attempted to learn Polish.

In December 2011, after graduating early, Caroline couldn't wait for the next advenuture and took off for San Francisco. She currently works as the communications intern at Net Impact and battles the impossible hills on her bicycle.

She'll zip back to Pittsburgh in May because she was selected as Carnegie Mellon's Student Commencement Speaker. In June, she'll again head back east to write more poems than she'll know what to do with.
